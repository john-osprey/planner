# Increase Soluble Fiber Intake

Lower LDL cholesterol by increasing soluble fiber consumption to 10-15g per day through strategic food additions.

## Quick Links

- [Full Project Details](increase-soluble-fiber.md)

## Project Status

**Start Date**: 2025-11-30
**Status**: Active - Baseline Phase

## Goal

Increase soluble fiber to 10-15g per day to reduce LDL cholesterol by 5-10%

## The "Big 4" High-Fiber Foods

1. **Oats** - 2g per cup cooked oatmeal
2. **Beans** - 2-3g per 1/2 cup
3. **Apples** - 1-1.5g per apple (with skin)
4. **Oranges** - 2-3g per orange

## Daily Routine

**Breakfast**: Oatmeal with apple slices (5-6g fiber)
**Lunch/Dinner**: Add beans 4-5x/week (2-3g per serving)
**Snacks**: Apple or orange daily (1-3g)
**Extras**: Chia/flax seeds, vegetables, berries

## Weekly Shopping List

- Oatmeal or oat bran
- Canned beans (black, kidney, chickpeas)
- Apples (7-10)
- Oranges (7-10)
- Ground flaxseed
- Chia seeds

## Important Reminders

- **Increase gradually** - Add 2-3g every few days
- **Drink more water** - 64+ oz daily (fiber needs water)
- **Be patient** - Some initial gas/bloating is normal
- **Track progress** - Use app to monitor fiber intake
